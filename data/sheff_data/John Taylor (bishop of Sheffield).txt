Born on 13 November 1912 and educated at Hymers College and The Queen's College, Oxford, he was ordained in 1937 and began his career with  a curacy at Walcot, Bath.  He was then a Tutor, Lecturer and Chaplain at Wycliffe Hall, Oxford after which he was Vicar of Christ Church, Claughton, Merseyside. From 1954, he was Principal of  Wycliffe, a post he held until his elevation to the Episcopate . He died in post on 4 July 1971 aged 58.
